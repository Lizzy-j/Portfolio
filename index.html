About

I have recently graduated from the Founders and Coders programme. Before moving into software development, I previously worked in Content Design and project management. Thanks to these experiences, I recognise the importance of developing tech that is accessible to all, irrespective of their backgrounds. Working for the parliamentary digital service gave me the opportunity to develop tools to ensure the general public is aware of what’s going politics-wise. After spending two years, I decided to take the plunge and embark on a career in programming.

My university degree in Comparative Literature and French has given me the foundation for programming due to the level of literary and linguistics study required for a degree. Despite not being traditional for most developers, it has made me feel confident in picking up new skills and technologies quickly.

I’m looking forward to joining a team where I can use my user-centred design background and technical skills to make a difference. When I’m not tinkering away on my laptop, I enjoy attending meetups such as Codebar and Ladies of Code, or with my nose in a book.

Main projects

Criminal Appeals Fund

Dev Ops Lead and Developer

As part of the Tech for Better programme organised by Founders and Coders, I worked in a team of 4 to develop a single-page web application for the Criminal Appeals Fund. The core aim of the platform was to provide legal professionals with a means to apply for funding in the criminal justice sector. The app was developed using React, Material UI and Style Components in the front-end. As Dev Ops lead, I was responsible for implementing serverless architecture into the project, deploying the app with Netlify lambda functions and setting up an Airtable database for the project’s backend.

Where's Whippy

UX/UI Lead and Developer

A single-page web application connecting local ice cream vendors with customers using the Google Maps API. The project is built with React and Styled Components in the front-end. Whilst the back-end was built with PostgreSQL Express.js. I was responsible for the implementation of the Google Maps API to render geolocation data of customers for vendors. As the UX lead, I led user testing and ensured the app met the accessibility guidelines outlined by W3C.
